Get to Know a Developer: Casey Gum

I’m so happy to finally have another new developer to write about!  Casey Gum just joined the team as a Principal Consultant yesterday, and so far he’s doing great with jumping right in to working with clients. He’s got a pretty full schedule already, but he spared me a few moments this morning.

Casey is our first new Principal Consultant at Headspring since Alonso Robles came on board in December, and boy did we need him for all the big projects we have taken on in 2012.  He comes from 8 years spent in tech consulting for military intelligence and law enforcement with Textron.  He is also one of the original founders of Austin’s FG Squared.

Here’s a bit of our conversation:

Are you an expert at anything?

C# in general, Silverlight and WPF.  Other than that, I would merely categorize myself as proficient in a wide variety of technologies.

Who is on your list of ideal clients to work for or individuals to work with?

My ideal client is the one that can most benefit from my expertise.  As far as individuals, I like working with anyone with a good attitude and a passion for learning.

What led you to software development?

I have always loved computers. I knew from a very early age that I would be an engineer or a programmer.

What did you learn first?

The first language I learned was BASIC on a TI-99 in 1981.  I saved/loaded my (mostly Dungeons and Dragons-related) programs to a cassette tape.  Good times, good times.

Do you like teaching?

I do like teaching. I actually taught my first computer class to kids at Computerland in El Paso when I was 15.  I have taught a number of developer training sessions on Design Principles, Patterns and Practices.

What are your plans for the future?

For the next 10-12 years, I am going to do everything I can to prepare my children for a career in engineering. We need more engineers.
