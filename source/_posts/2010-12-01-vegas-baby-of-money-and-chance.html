If you don't collect feedback you're never going to know if you're executing your development plans successfully. What one is fighting with code reviews is entropy. A system, left to its own devices, will naturally breakdown into a state of stagnation. At Headspring, we have architecture and governing processes in place to make sure our systems remain vibrant, alive, dynamic, and continually able to change, but without the feedback process we run the risk of allowing chance to run the life of our systems. Let's take a look at one of the oldest industries that we are aware of: The Finance Industry. Money has been around for a long time and the art of keeping track of money has been around for a long time too. That's finance.
The software industry, the automating of business processes and functions, is relatively new in the grand scheme of things. In contrast, in the finance world, a lot of the processes and practices are very mature, and reviewing "the books," reviewing financial statements and auditing financial statements, is a common practice regardless of the trust that we place on the people we have maintaining the books with their own companies.
  • There is a standing practice that is a best practice in the financial world: You get your books reviewed. You have a schedule for when you're going to have your books audited and you make sure that the numbers line up... that they reconcile how you expect them to reconcile.
  • In the software world, that's not as much a widely expected practice, and unfortunately the absence of review is often where things start to go wrong.

The Obvious, Easy Solution

Let's go ahead and take a look. Let's see whether the system is performing or not, whether it is scaling or if we have huge bottlenecks in some areas. Let's do a code review and try to find some help for ourselves. We may decide it is time to divest a product line or sell a software system or perhaps purchase another company, and it could only be considered due diligence to assess the value of the particular systems in play by way of code reviews.
